Employment Opportunities

Membership and Communications Manager
Women's Bar Association

The WBA is seeking a Membership and Communications Manager. This full-time position is responsible for managing the operational aspects of recruiting and retaining WBA members, working with volunteers to assist with the execution of programs and events throughout the year, and managing member communications such as email alerts and social media postings.

--------------

Assistant Legal Director
Community Legal Services and Counseling Center

Community Legal Services and Counseling Center (CLSACC) seeks a highly organized, detail-oriented, self-motivated professional who has a strong belief in the mission and principles of the organization and committed to assisting the most vulnerable, particularly during these uncertain and challenging times. Reporting to the Legal Director, the Assistant Legal Director will assist in supervising and coordinating the operations of the agency's legal program. CLSACC has been growing over the past several years and soon will be moving to a new location in Cambridge.

--------------

Organizing Manager
Planned Parenthood Advocacy Fund

The Planned Parenthood Advocacy Fund of Massachusetts (PPAF) seeks a highly motivated and detail oriented experienced organizer to lead its organizing team. The Organizing Manager manages the organizing team within the Public Affairs Department. The Organizing Manager is responsible for developing and implementing a statewide strategy to harness the energy of supporters across the Commonwealth, building the organization’s base and overseeing PPAF’s outreach programs.  
 

Beyond the WBA

 

Going Global: Exploring Careers in International Law
May 18, Northeastern University

Please join Northeastern University for Going Global: Exploring Careers in International Law, a panel discussion and complimentary lunch on Thursday, May 18th, 2017 at Northeastern University School of Law. This program is a precursor to the annual Women in the Law Conference on Friday, May 19th.

The world is a big place—full of all sorts of opportunities to use your law degree to cross borders and boundaries. Come to this panel to hear about the many different ways to build a career in international law. Lee Zak ’82, director of the U.S. Trade and Development Agency from 2009 to 2017, will moderate a panel of accomplished alumni/ae attorneys whose practices stretch around the globe.
